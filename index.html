Lamborghini Aventador SVJ

The Aventador powertrain features a mid-mounted naturally aspirated 6.5-liter V-12 that develops 769 horsepower. The engine's 531 pound-feet of torque makes its way to all four wheels via a seven-speed automated-manual transmission. It produces 759 horsepower and 531 lb-ft of torque. It does 0-60 mph in 2.8 seconds with a top speed of 217 mph.

McLaren Senna

The base model of the McLaren Senna is motivated by a twin-turbocharged 4.0-liter V-8 that generates 789 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque. It also does 0-60 mph in just 2.8 seconds. The regular Senna has a top speed of 208 miles per hour.

Ford GT

The new Ford GT is equipped with a twin-turbocharged 3.5-liter V-6 engine, pumping out 660 horsepower and 550 pound-feet of torque. The V-6 is mated to a seven-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission. It can go from 0-60 mph in a 3 seconds flat and has a top speed of 216 mph.

Mercedes-Benz C63s AMG

Mercedes-Benz C63 S has a hand-built, twin-turbo 4.0-liter V-8 engine. It makes 503 horsepower and 516 lb-ft and uses a nine-speed automatic with rear-wheel drive. Top speed of the AMG C63 S is electronically limited to 180 mph and it goes from zero to 60 mph in 3.7 seconds.

BMW M4 competition

The M4 is motivated by a twin-turbocharged 3.0-liter inline-six that produces 503 horses and 479 pound-feet of torque. The M4 competition package comes with an eight-speed automatic transmission and a rear-biased all-wheel-drive system. BMW claims this beast can do 0-60 mph in 3.6 seconds with a top speed of 180 mph.

Jeep Grand Cherokee Trackhawk

With a 707-hp supercharged Hellcat V-8 under the hood, it's the most powerful Jeep ever built. Trackhawk's all-wheel-drive traction and super responsive throttle allows the Jeep to virtually teleport to 60 mph in 3.4 seconds, which even beats most of the performance cars.

Ford Mustang Shelby GT500

Aptly called Predator, the motor is a supercharged 5.2-liter V-8 that summons 760 horsepower and 625 pound-feet of torque. A seven-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission pushes all that power to the rear wheels to show its presence on the road. It does 0-60 mph in 3.4 just like the Camaro ZL1 but lacks in the top speed segment with only 180 mph.

Chevrolet Camaro ZL1

The Camaro's supercharged 6.2-liter V-8 is a well-behaved and tractable beast that makes power all over the rev range. Whether paired with the standard six-speed manual transmission or the available 10-speed automatic, the supercharged small-block V-8 lays down stupidly incredible performance figures. Top speed: 190 mph and 0-60 mph: 3.4 seconds.

RAM 1500 TRX

TRX, the underdog of the trucks, carries a supercharged 6.2-liter Hemi V-8 that produces 702 horsepower and 650 lb-ft of torque, which is sent to all four wheels through an eight-speed automatic transmission. It shoots to 60 mph in just 3.7 seconds and completes the quarter-mile in 12.3 seconds at 110 mph. It is the quickest stock pickup truck available.
